The Practice

Writing as healing

In these workshops, the focus is on process, not product. Participants write from a generative prompt that invites sensory and memory work, reflection, and integration — then share their experiences, and, if it feels right, what they've written.

Research consistently shows that expressive writing releases stress, reduces symptoms of chronic illness, and improves both physical and emotional wellbeing. Emma's workshops draw on this evidence base and weave it with the traditions of creative nonfiction — the personal essay, the lyric meditation, the memoir fragment.

Participants do not need to consider themselves writers. They do not need prior experience with journaling or creative writing. They need only a willingness to put something true on the page.

Research-backed benefits

✦

Stress reduction — expressive writing lowers cortisol and measurably reduces stress responses

✦

Chronic illness relief — writing about difficult experiences reduces symptom severity in conditions including asthma, arthritis, and autoimmune disease

✦

Emotional processing — narrative-making helps the brain integrate difficult memories and experiences

✦

Community and connection — shared writing creates authentic bonds between participants

✦

Improved physical health — Pennebaker's foundational research links journaling to stronger immune function
