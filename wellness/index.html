Teaching & workshops

Writing for Wellness

I direct the Writing for Wellness initiative with the Butler University Master of Fine Arts in Creative Writing program, and lead generative workshops both virtually and in the Indianapolis community.

The practice

In my writing for wellness workshops, the focus is on process, not product. We write from a generative prompt that invites sensory and memory work, reflection, and integration, then share our experiences and — if it feels right — share what we've written.

Research consistently shows that writing in this way releases stress, reduces symptoms of chronic illness, and improves physical and emotional health. Participants don't need to consider themselves writers. They need only a willingness to put something true on the page.

Research-backed benefits

  • 01
    Stress reduction

    Expressive writing lowers cortisol and measurably reduces stress responses.

  • 02
    Chronic illness relief

    Writing about difficult experiences reduces symptom severity in conditions including asthma, arthritis, and autoimmune disease.

  • 03
    Emotional processing

    Narrative-making helps the brain integrate difficult memories and experiences.

  • 04
    Community & connection

    Shared writing creates authentic bonds between participants.

  • 05
    Improved physical health

    Pennebaker's foundational research links journaling to stronger immune function.
